# MIL 104: Presidio Basic Intelligence Gathering Doctrine - The Eyes and Ears of the Earth

This foundational course establishes the Presidio's doctrine for Basic Intelligence Gathering. It focuses on the ethical and effective collection of raw information from diverse sources. This is the essential first step in understanding the global environment and providing the raw material for all subsequent analysis.

### Part I: Core Philosophy - Seeing Clearly, Listening Deeply

In a world saturated with information, the most critical skill is not just to collect data, but to discern what is truly relevant and reliable. The Presidio views basic intelligence gathering as an act of **attentive observation**—seeing clearly and listening deeply to the subtle signals of the global ecosystem.

Your goal is to provide an unbiased, unvarnished picture of reality, free from preconception or agenda. You are the eyes and ears of the Earth, providing the ground truth upon which all wise decisions are made. This requires a commitment to **Information Verification** and the understanding that **Community Trust** is paramount for effective, ethical collection.

### Part II: The Four Pillars of Basic Collection

1.  **Source Verification & Information Verification:** Every piece of information is only as good as its source. You are trained in rigorous source verification techniques, assessing credibility, access, and potential biases. You implement processes to verify the accuracy of information, cross-checking data from multiple independent sources, and establishing credibility standards for all intelligence. You combat misinformation through rigorous fact-checking at the collection point.

2.  **Ethical Collection & Community Trust:** Your methods are always lawful and ethical. You do not engage in illegal surveillance, coercion, or deception when collecting raw intelligence. Your integrity is paramount, and compromising it for a piece of information makes that information tainted and unreliable. You build networks based on trust and transparency, developing relationships that withstand pressure and uncertainty, and cultivating reliability through consistent ethical behavior.

3.  **Objective Reporting:** Raw intelligence must be reported objectively, without interpretation or analysis. Your role as a collector is to present the facts as they are, leaving the synthesis and judgment to the analysts. This prevents the introduction of bias at the earliest stage of the intelligence cycle, ensuring the purity of the data.

4.  **Operational Security (OPSEC) & Digital Security:** Protecting the collector and the collected information is vital. You are trained in basic OPSEC principles, including compartmentalizing information on a need-to-know basis, establishing secure meeting protocols, and implementing counter-surveillance measures. For digital collection, this includes employing end-to-end encryption for communications, using secure operating systems, and practicing digital hygiene to protect sources and methods.

### Part III: Basic Collection Methods

Presidio officers are trained in a range of fundamental intelligence gathering methods, often employing decentralized approaches to enhance resilience and security:

*   **Human Intelligence (HUMINT):** The collection of information from human sources. This includes interviewing, debriefing, and building trusted relationships within communities. Emphasis is placed on active listening, cultural sensitivity, and ethical engagement. This often involves "Scout Duties" such as observing and documenting activities, gathering information on resource movements, and collecting data on the impact on civilian populations.

*   **Signals Intelligence (SIGINT):** The collection of information from electronic signals. This includes monitoring open-source communications, identifying patterns in digital traffic, and understanding basic network architectures. You learn to distinguish signal from noise in the digital realm. Secure transmission methods, including encrypted digital communications and authentication protocols, are paramount.

*   **Open Source Intelligence (OSINT):** The systematic collection of information from publicly available sources. This includes media monitoring, social media analysis, academic research, and public records. You are trained to navigate vast amounts of public data to extract relevant insights, tracking social media for patterns and anomalies, and analyzing news reports for inconsistencies or propaganda. "Scout Decentralization" is key here, utilizing distributed networks of observers and maintaining separation between collection cells.

*   **Geospatial Intelligence (GEOINT) & Field Observation:** The collection of information derived from imagery and mapping, combined with direct observation of physical activities and movements. This includes interpreting satellite imagery, aerial photography, and topographical maps to understand physical terrain, infrastructure, and activity patterns. You track infrastructure changes, security measures, and patterns of enforcement and control, identifying vulnerable points in systems through direct field observation.

### Conclusion: The Foundation of Wisdom

Basic intelligence gathering is the bedrock upon which all other Presidio operations are built. Without accurate, ethically collected, and objectively reported raw information, advanced analysis is impossible, and strategic decisions are blind. It is the initial step in transforming raw data into actionable intelligence.

You are trained to be meticulous observers, discerning listeners, and unwavering reporters of truth. You are the essential first line in our quest for wisdom, ensuring that the Presidio always operates from a position of clarity and understanding.
