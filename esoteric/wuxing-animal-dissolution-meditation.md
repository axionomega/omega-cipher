# Wuxing Animal Dissolution Meditation

> Meditation on the five elements of the body is an old tradition which is used primarily to loosen the grasping at the self. In Tai Chi these elements take the form of animals and their corresponding organs. The dissolution of the elements/organs should be done in both creation and destructive cycles in you meditations. The creation cycle is air, water, wood, fire and earth. The destructive cycle is in the reverse order: earth, fire, wood, water and air. The destructive cycle is what I will present here because it is the order that is practiced in the form.

---

### The Destructive Cycle Chart

| Element | Animal | Organ   | Activity               |
| :------ | :----- | :------ | :--------------------- |
| 1. Earth  | Dragon | Spleen  | Harmonizes all organs  |
| 2. Fire   | Tiger  | Heart   | Creates Heat           |
| 3. Wood   | Monkey | Liver   | Nourishes skin         |
| 4. Water  | Bear   | Kidneys | Nourishes Blood        |
| 5. Air    | Eagle  | Lungs   | Opens the body         |

*This chart should be reviewed before you begin this meditation. Remember as well this meditation is done to revitalize the organs which can be done standing or sitting.*

---

## Preparation: Dissolving the Gross Body

To begin this meditation, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and parallel, with your torso centered evenly between them. The knees are straight but not locked, and the arms are at your sides about two inches away from the body.

First, relax the body. Go through it mentally and ask the places you feel tension to dissolve like water turning to steam. If you keep your body perfectly upright, this will help you stay relaxed.

When the gross body is dissolved, you can then dissolve the tightness in your meridians, and eventually in the points along the meridians as well. Feel the top of your head become light and sensitive, and feel your center dropping into your root. This should allow your body to feel very active and alive.

## The Meditation Cycle

While your mind is resting quietly in this relaxed state, begin the cycle.

### 1. Earth (Dragon / Spleen)

Begin by feeling the heavy parts of your body: the bones and flesh. These heavy parts are the **Earth** elements, which represent the **Dragon**. Dragon also represents the center of a spiral because of its spiral-like nature. This ability to radiate from the center is precisely what the **Spleen** does; it regulates the organs around it.

In your meditations, feel your spleen radiate outwards, purifying all of your organs with white light or steam. At first, you will notice some darkness or vagueness in feeling your organs. This is normal. Try to persist until your whole body has become filled with light. You can use the breath to guide the light or simply visualize it radiating through your body.

When you feel the heavy parts of your body turn very light, this means the Earth element has dissolved and the organs have been harmonized. When this is very clear in your mind, it is time to move on.

### 2. Fire (Tiger / Heart)

Check in with your body. Make sure you are relaxed and your mind is calm before beginning. The lightness of your body allows the beating of your **Heart** to become very pronounced. At the same time, your body becomes warm and craves movement. This is the element **Fire**, or **Tiger**.

Continue your meditation by focusing on the heat in the center of your heart and feel that radiate outwards towards your extremities. The body should increase in warmth and feel very active but relaxed. When the warmth is felt throughout the body evenly and even into the skin, the next meditation will begin.

### 3. Wood (Monkey / Liver)

Again, ensure the first two meditations are still present through feeling in your body. Bring your awareness of warmth to the right side of your abdomen and allow the warmth to enter the **Liver**. The element **Wood** is related to the skin, which is now the most external part of our bodies. This skin is related to wood because of its transformative nature. The liver is one of the most flexible organs and can help us adapt. Likewise, **Monkeys** can easily adapt to living in trees or on the ground.

In your meditations, make sure you feel your liver radiate out and nourish all parts of your skin, from the top to the bottom of your body. When you feel the skin as an organ completely unified, it is time to move on.

### 4. Water (Bear / Kidneys)

The **Water** element arises as your meditation on the skin dissolves into the water beneath it: your blood. **Bear** is related to water because of its heavy nature and the way a bear moves supports the **Kidneys**. The kidneys are close to your lower back on either side of the spine. The meditation on water is to purify the blood, which is the activity of the kidneys.

To do this, visualize the blood moving through the kidneys like warm steam filling a sauna. Again, notice where the blood is not moving evenly and bring awareness to that area through the steam visualization. Once the blood is aroused in this way, the heat penetrates all the organ systems and makes one aware of the liquid part of our bodies. Once the whole body is unified through this practice, you move to the next meditation.

### 5. Air (Eagle / Lungs)

Each element you meditate on is in order of its subtlety. This serves to make your mind also more relaxed and subtle, allowing it to penetrate deeper into pure awareness without discursive thought. The most subtle element is **Air**, which is arrived at when the element of water dissolves into steam and your consciousness becomes spacious. The element air is related to **Eagle**, which rides on air and purifies one's **Lungs**.

The purification is done by allowing the air element to fill up your body completely like a balloon. Imagine your body is an empty container just filled up with air. When you can breathe and feel the air go through every pore of your body, you can move on.

---

## Final Purification: Dissolving the World

The last meditation purifies the external world. Once your body has been purified completely, you need to purify the environment. This is done by visualizing the Hexagrams of the I Ching as you walk the circle in the form.

In your meditation, imagine that the hexagrams make up your external world and at the same time purify your perception of it, making it a pure realm. When the internal world of your body has been purified and the external world has become unified as a pure vision, then dissolve the external world:
- Earth dissolves into Fire.
- Fire dissolves into Wood.
- Wood dissolves into Water.
- Water dissolves into Air.

Dissolve your body into space and the external world with it. Rest in the state beyond elements and thought; rest in pure, naked awareness.

## Coming Out of Meditation

When you are ready to come out, recreate the elements in the reverse order presented here (the Creation Cycle), starting with Air and moving all the way up to Earth.
