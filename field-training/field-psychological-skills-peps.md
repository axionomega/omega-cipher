# Field Doctrine: Performance-Enhancing Psychological Skills (PEPS)

**Document ID:** PEPS-FIELD-01
**Classification:** Standard Issue
**Issuing Authority:** Cypress Hall / Bingshen Collective Joint Command
**Subject:** Immediate Psychological Readiness for High-Stress Encounters

---

## 1. Overview

This document outlines the **PEPS framework**, a set of four simple, actionable psychological skills designed for real-time use immediately before or during a high-stress event. This is not a replacement for deep conditioning (SIT, Somatic Regulation) but rather the field-ready application of those principles.

The framework is remembered by the mnemonic **BTSF**: **Breathe, Talk, See, Focus**. It is designed to be recalled and deployed in seconds to mitigate stress, enhance cognitive function, and ensure optimal performance when it matters most.

---

## 2. The Four Skills (BTSF)

### Skill 1: BREATHE (Arousal Control)

This is your emergency brake for the body's panic response. When you feel the spike of adrenaline—the pounding heart, the knot in the stomach—you immediately engage in **Tactical Breathing**.

-   **Technique:** Box Breathing.
    1.  Inhale slowly for a 4-count, pulling the breath deep into your abdomen.
    2.  Hold your breath for a 4-count.
    3.  Exhale slowly and completely for a 4-count.
    4.  Hold the lungs empty for a 4-count.
-   **Application:** Use this before entering a hostile area, while approaching a target, or in any brief pause in action. It is the fastest way to consciously regulate your autonomic nervous system.

### Skill 2: TALK (Cognitive Control)

Engage in positive, structured self-talk to direct your mindset. Your internal monologue is a tool; use it to build confidence and provide clear instructions.

-   **Guidelines:**
    -   Keep phrases short and specific.
    -   Use the first person ("I") and present tense ("am," "can").
    -   Be positive (e.g., "I am calm," not "I am not nervous").
    -   Speak with intent and kindness to yourself.
-   **Examples:**
    -   **Motivational:** "I am trained for this." "I can handle this." "They need my help, and I will succeed."
    -   **Instructional:** "Slow is smooth, smooth is fast." "Focus on the front sight." "Check your corners."

### Skill 3: SEE (Mental Rehearsal)

Before a planned action, run a "mental movie" of the procedure. Visualization activates the same neural pathways as physical action, programming your mind and body for success. For maximum effect, use the **PETTLEP** model for your imagery.

-   **The PETTLEP Model:**
    -   **P**hysical: Imagine the physical feelings—the weight of your gear, the grip on a tool.
    -   **E**nvironment: Visualize the specifics of the location—the lighting, the sounds, the smells.
    -   **T**ask: Rehearse the specific sequence of the task, from start to finish.
    -   **T**iming: Imagine the task at the correct speed, not rushed.
    -   **L**earning: Incorporate lessons from past training into your visualization.
    -   **E**motion: Imagine the feeling of calm confidence during the task and successful completion.
    -   **P**erspective: See the rehearsal through your own eyes (first-person).

### Skill 4: FOCUS (Attention Funnel)

This is the final switch you flip before execution. Use a pre-chosen **"trigger word"** to activate your selective attention, funneling your focus onto the critical task at hand and filtering out distractions.

-   **The Technique:**
    1.  Choose a simple, powerful word that resonates with you (e.g., "Engage," "Smooth," "Focus," "Now").
    2.  Practice using this word in training simulations as the final mental step before action.
    3.  In the field, take a final breath, speak your trigger word to yourself (aloud or mentally), and execute the action.
-   **Application:** The trigger word acts as a cognitive signal flare, cutting through the noise of stress and initiating a state of one-pointed concentration. It is the final "go" signal from your mind to your body.
